Point Process Application to Extreme CO2 Readings
In this project, completed in my Honours at UCT, my associate Manon Shapiro and I explored the application of Point Process models to extreme CO2 readings. The modelling of extreme events poses a particular challenge as many modern schools of thought, such as Extreme Value Theory, assume independence between event occurrences. But if you consider the example of an earthquake, in which the aftershocks of one earthquake can trigger another, it is clear that this assumption does not hold in many real-life scenarios. We looked at Point Processes and various extensions as a means of accounting for the ‘self-exciting’ nature of many extreme readings. All models, plots and validation techniques were produced using R. Click the link below to check out my Github repository!
